Waleed's outreach credibility does not come from grade-outcome data. He has none until August 2026 and never implies otherwise. It comes from having delivered at scale for names bigger than his:

- **The Medic Life:** ran an interview lecture series that took over 250 students through in 10 weeks.
- **PasTest:** partnered with PasTest, the larger brand carrying his work.
- **r/A-levels:** ran free workshops for r/A-levels (r/alevel, 201,660 members as of July 2026), the largest A-level community on Reddit and Discord, with outstanding reviews. The claim is true and approved; note for accuracy in conversation that the sub's audience is largely international CAIE students (see `content/reddit/2026-07-15-reddit-verdict-and-plan.md`), so frame it as community scale and delivery proof, not as UK reach.
- **Dr Waleed Ahmad, MBBS:** practising NHS foundation-year doctor, former top-performing A-level student who got into medicine. It is his method, taught by him. Never a marketplace of tutors.
- Worked with 1,000+ A-level students (approved figure).
- Session confidence ratings: across the recent 12-week Biology and Chemistry Accelerators, average topic confidence rose from 6.2 to 8.3 out of 10 (74 responses). Use sparingly and only where a number genuinely helps.

## What Waleed can put on the table

- **Live teaching in his proven format:** free webinars, workshop series, masterclasses. His strongest asset: the delivery record above is exactly this shape.
- **The free tools:** the Revision Diagnostic (/revision-diagnostic, 20 questions, emailed report), the Revision Tracker (/revision-tracker, topic audit and week builder), the Parent Guide PDF (/parents), the Blurting Template. Offerable as links, features, co-branded pages or embedded recommendations.
- **Written content:** definitive how-to-revise pieces, results-day support content, parent-facing guides, in his voice with his byline.
- **A face and authority:** a practising doctor who teaches study method. Many resource businesses have content but no expert front-person on the method side.
- **The Sunday Session:** a weekly student newsletter for reciprocal features.
- **Programme delivery** (Summer Accelerator, Subject Accelerators, Study System) where genuinely right for the partner. Rarely the lead offer: it reads as "sell my course" unless the partner asked for it.

## What he wants back (acceptable returns)

Audience access is the prize. Cash sponsorship is not the play.

- A dedicated send or feature in the partner's newsletter.
- Name, logo and link placement on their site.
- Co-promoted registrations where sign-ups consent to hear from him, so his MailerLite list grows.
- A recurring content slot with byline and link.
- Featured placement of his free tools in their resource roundups.
- Referral or revenue-share arrangements only once trust exists, never in the opening ask.

## Red lines

- Nothing sends without Waleed. Ever.
- No grade-outcome claims and no hinting at them.
- No marketplace framing of his business.
- His MailerLite list and signup flows are load-bearing (the tracker and diagnostic automations hang off them). Never promise a partner student data, list swaps, or changes to his signup flows without Waleed agreeing the exact mechanics first. Consent stays explicit on any shared registration.
- He is one doctor with a hospital rota. Never promise weekly commitments or scale he cannot staff: a series of one to three live sessions, planned around his shifts, is the honest ceiling for live delivery.
- Co-branding yes, disappearing no. Do not offer to white-label his tools or teaching in a way that removes his name: the name and the method are the long game.
