# Deck blueprints

Three skeletons. Pick one, write the slide list first, then build. A deck that follows a blueprint can still be wrong if the facts are stale: collect facts fresh every time.

## 1. Programme sales deck (9 to 10 slides)

For a live call selling one of the paid programmes to a parent (usually with the student present). The parent is the buyer: credibility and safety carry as much weight as the offer.

| # | Slide | Archetype | Job |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cover | Cover (dark) | Name the programme, the promise, the start date |
| 2 | At a glance | Tiles | The whole offer in six facts including "from" price |
| 3 | The problem | Split | Why this exists; the cost of doing nothing |
| 4 | Who built it | Split + photo | Dr Waleed MBBS, 1,000+ students, tutor credentials |
| 5 | The method | Statement + steps | Diagnose, Rebuild, Coach |
| 6 | Inside it | Split | Session anatomy or weekly rhythm; what is included |
| 7 | Proof | Proof | Approved stats with honest captions, two verbatim quotes |
| 8 | Pricing | Pricing | All options, featured tier, value anchor, risk-free line |
| 9 | Close | Close (dark) | Three next steps, date chip, scarcity fact, contact |

Facts to collect before building: start date, duration, session cadence and length, subjects, exam boards, who teaches, recordings policy, clash policy, every price and saving, the guarantee wording, what happens after the programme, current approved proof points.

## 2. Workshop deck (7 to 9 slides)

For pitching or opening a one-off workshop (school, parent group, online event). Same skeleton with these changes: slide 2 tiles cover date, length, audience, format, capacity and price (or free); slide 6 becomes the agenda (three to five gold-numbered rows, each with a takeaway, not a topic); slide 8 becomes logistics (what attendees need, how to book) and drops pricing cards if the workshop is free. If the workshop does not exist yet, get date, venue or platform, length, capacity, price and the promised takeaway from Waleed before building; none of those may be assumed.

## 3. Partnership deck (8 to 10 slides)

For showing a prospective partner who we are and proposing a specific trade. Decision-maker audience: scan-first, zero hype, every number dated. Build only after (or alongside) the `partnership-outreach` skill's dossier for the target; the dossier is the fact source for their side.

| # | Slide | Archetype | Job |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cover | Cover (dark) | "A-Level Accelerators x [Partner]", one-line premise |
| 2 | Who we are | Tiles | Six facts: founder, method, programmes, audience assets, list size (dated), channels |
| 3 | What we have built | Split | The product suite and free-tool funnel, screenshots or motif |
| 4 | Who we reach | Proof | Audience and engagement numbers, each with its date and source |
| 5 | Why [Partner] | Split | What we admire or use of theirs; why the audiences fit (from the dossier) |
| 6 | The trade | Statement + steps | What we bring, what we ask, why it is fair; the designed trade from the dossier |
| 7 | What it could look like | Split | The first concrete joint step, small and reversible |
| 8 | Close | Close (dark) | Next step (a call or a pilot), Waleed's contact |

Partnership rules: audience or subscriber numbers must carry their as-of date; nothing about the partner that is not in the sourced dossier; no revenue projections; the ask on slide 6 must match the ask in the outreach email so the deck and email never disagree.

## Copy voice on slides

Slides carry fragments and statements, not paragraphs. Where a full sentence appears (ledes, founder slide, statements) it is Waleed's spoken voice: direct address, short emphatic sentences, contractions, medical framing where natural. The founder slide paragraph is condensed from his real story on the homepage; do not write him a new biography. Numbers are set in serif, claims in plain sans, and anything the deck cannot prove stays off the deck.
