The diagnosis

It's a retrieval failure, not a knowledge failure.

The knowledge went in. You just never practised getting it out. Recognising your notes and recalling them cold are two different skills.

I'm a doctor. I can't treat a patient until I know what's wrong. Name the real problem and the fix becomes obvious: train retrieval.

Step 1 of 4 · Blurting

Study the topic properly. Once.

Twenty minutes with the notes, textbook or video. Full attention, phone in another room.

This is the last time this session you're allowed to look at them.

Step 2 of 4 · Blurting

Close everything. Blank page. Write it all.

Every fact, diagram, equation and link you can pull from memory. No peeking. Peeking turns it back into recognition and wastes the whole exercise.

It will feel uncomfortable. That discomfort is where the marks are made.

Step 4 of 4 · Blurting

Blurt the same topic again in two days.

Same blank page, same rules. Watch the red shrink. That shrinking red is the needle actually moving, and you can see it happen.

Quality over quantity. Two focused blurts beat six hours of re-reading.
