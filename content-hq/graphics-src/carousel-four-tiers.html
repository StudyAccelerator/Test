Recall: knowing it isn't retrieving it.

Reading your notes and nodding along is tier 1. The exam hall gives you a blank page and a ticking clock.

If you only ever meet the content with the book open, the first time you try to retrieve it cold will be the real exam.

Stuck here looks like: "I knew it yesterday, it's gone today." That's a retrieval failure, not a knowledge failure. Train retrieval.

3

Application: the exam never asks it the way you learned it.

You revised osmosis. The question is about a farmer's crops in a drought. Same content, unfamiliar costume.

Application is a skill you build by doing unfamiliar questions, not by reading familiar notes harder.

Stuck here looks like: "I understand everything in class but lose marks on every paper." You know it. You can't use it yet.

4

Exam mastery: playing the examiner's game.

Command words, mark allocation, timing, the exact phrases the mark scheme pays for. The A* students aren't smarter. They've studied how the paper pays out marks.

In medicine we'd call this reading the test, not just the textbook.

Stuck here looks like: "I wrote loads and got 2 out of 6." You answered the topic. You didn't answer the question.
