01
METHOD
Is your revision retrieval, or recognition dressed up as work?

Re-reading feels like learning. The page looks familiar, so your brain files it as known.

But exams never ask what looks familiar. They ask what you can produce from a blank page, under time.

The check: close the notes, take a blank page, write the topic from memory. The gaps you find are your real revision list.

02
RETENTION
Does last month's revision still exist in your head?

Memory fades on a curve. Revise a topic once and most of it is gone within weeks. That's not a flaw, it's how every brain works.

Top students don't have better memories. They have return visits: next day, a few days later, a week on.

The check: pick a topic you "finished" in spring. Blank page, ten minutes. What's actually still there?
Dr Waleed Ahmad, MBBSalevelaccelerators.com
03
EXAM CRAFT
Can you turn what you know into marks, under time?

Knowing it and scoring it are different skills. You can understand a topic completely and still lose half the marks on it.

Command words. Mark scheme phrasing. Timing. None of it is on the syllabus. All of it decides your grade.

The check: after your next past paper, count the marks you lost on things you actually knew. That number is your exam craft gap.
Dr Waleed Ahmad, MBBSalevelaccelerators.com
04
TARGETING
Do your hours go where the marks are missing?

A topic you already score 80 percent on has almost no marks left to give you. Revising it again feels great and buys nothing.

The grade you want is sitting, nearly whole, inside the topics you keep avoiding.

The check: name the topic you've dodged all year. If you can name it instantly, your hours are going to the wrong places.
Dr Waleed Ahmad, MBBSalevelaccelerators.com
05
CONSISTENCY
Does the work happen without a deadline forcing it?

Bursts feel heroic. But the forgetting curve loves bursts: cram it this week, re-learn it from scratch next month.

A steady two hours beats a panicked ten. Every single time.

The check: if there were no test next week, would any revision happen at all? Honest answer only.
