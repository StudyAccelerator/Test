If your child is working hard and the grade still isn't moving, effort isn't the problem.

I'm a doctor. I can't treat a patient until I know what's wrong. Revision is no different, but almost nobody diagnoses it. They just tell your child to do more hours.

So I built a free diagnostic that does the diagnosing. It takes about 4 minutes. There's a version you can fill in about your child, and one they can do themselves.

It tells you which of the seven revision profiles they fall into, roughly how many hours a week are leaking, and what to do about it in the next 7 days.

It's completely free and there's nothing to buy.

## Post 2: the graduation photo (attach founder-card.png)

Six years ago I was sitting exactly where your child is sitting now.

I got into medicine, but my revision method was genuinely terrible. I made it on brute force. 12 hour days, notes copied out three times, a timetable that looked beautiful and did nothing. Nobody ever taught me how to actually revise. I'm guessing nobody has taught your child either.

That photo is graduation day. What it doesn't show is how much of that was wasted effort, and how much easier it would have been if someone had shown me the system instead of telling me to work harder.

That's why I teach it now. Not more hours. The system schools never get round to.

I'm an NHS doctor and I've taught over 1,000 A-level students. Any questions about your child's revision, just message me. I'm always happy to talk it through.
