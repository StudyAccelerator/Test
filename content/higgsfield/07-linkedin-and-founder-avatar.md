# 07. LinkedIn inserts and founder avatar scripts

Two health warnings first, both from the 16 July visual-guide correction:

1. **LinkedIn suppresses AI-looking content** (since May 2026) and real photos of Waleed outperform everything designed. So for LinkedIn, AI video is an insert, never the post: a two-second macro clip inside a real-footage or photo-led post, or a real-asset animation from file 05. The prompts in section A are chosen because they read as filmed stationery macro, the least AI-looking class of clip.
2. **The avatar is for Waleed's likeness only, from his own uploaded photo, used by him.** Best authenticity: record the script as a voice memo on his phone and use Lipsync Studio with his real voice; the text-to-speech route is the fallback. When a realistic generated person appears in a Meta ad, tick Meta's AI-generated media disclosure at publish time; that is Waleed's call at publishing, none of this auto-publishes.

Avatar setup for every script in section B: upload a sharp, warmly lit, front-facing photo of Waleed (plain background, shoulders up). Delivery: natural pace, warm, direct, slight smile, small natural head movement, no presenter energy. British English throughout.

## A. LinkedIn-safe insert clips (2 to 4 seconds inside photo-led posts)

**A1. Pen tap thinking**
```
Extreme macro, shallow focus: a purple pen taps twice on cream paper beside a half-finished handwritten list, then underlines the top item decisively. Warm desk light, paper grain, filmed-on-a-phone realism, writing stays soft and unreadable.
```
Format: 1:1 and 4:5, 3 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.

**A2. Flashcard flip beat**
```
Macro: one cream flashcard flips over in a hand under warm lamp light, pauses on the blank reverse, and a thumb taps it twice, thinking. Tactile, quiet, completely mundane in the best way. No text.
```
Format: 1:1 and 4:5, 3 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.

**A3. Stopwatch start**
```
Extreme macro: a thumb clicks the crown of an analogue stopwatch and the second hand sets off, crisp mechanical detail, warm light on brushed steel, shallow depth. Three seconds, one action.
```
Format: 1:1, 3 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.

**A4. Tea steam over notes**
```
Macro: steam curls from a mug across warm lamp light above an open notebook, the page edge lifting slightly, evening study atmosphere in one breath. Filmed-not-generated energy, soft grain, writing unreadable.
```
Format: 1:1 and 4:5, 4 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.

**A5. The circled date**
```
Macro: a hand circles a date on a paper wall calendar in purple ink, presses the pen home at the end of the circle, and lets go. Warm light, paper texture, numerals soft and unreadable so the overlay names the date.
```
Format: 1:1 and 4:5, 3 seconds. Tool: text-to-video. Pair with "Saturday 8 August" or "Thursday 13 August" as overlay text.

**A6. Stack squared**
```
Macro: two hands square a messy stack of A4 revision notes with three taps on the desk and set it down flush. Order restored in two seconds, warm window light, honest paper mess to honest paper order.
```
Format: 1:1, 3 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.

**A7. Envelope on the mat**
```
Macro from floor level: a white A4 envelope drops onto a British doormat in warm hallway light, bounces a centimetre, and settles face down. Results-season punctuation in two seconds. No readable text.
```
Format: 1:1 and 4:5, 3 seconds. Tool: text-to-video. Results week posts only.

**A8. Gold star pressed**
```
Extreme macro: a thumb presses a small gold foil star onto cream card and rolls once to seal it, the foil catching warm light. One tiny win, made physical. Three seconds.
```
Format: 1:1, 3 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.
