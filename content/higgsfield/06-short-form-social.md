# 06. Short-form social: TikTok and Reels b-roll library

Student-facing, all 9:16 vertical, all 6 to 10 seconds unless marked. These are cutaway shots and hooks to sit under Waleed's voiceover or between talking-head takes; the strategy stays face-led, AI clips are the garnish. Every people shot carries anti-AI-look language on purpose. Doctor characters are generic b-roll, never presented as Waleed.

---

## A. Scroll-stopping openers (the first 1.5 seconds)

**A1. Pen drop**
```
Extreme macro slow motion: a purple pen drops onto a wooden desk from above frame, bounces once with a sharp satisfying rattle, and rolls to a stop pointing at the camera. Warm lamp light, sharp focus, dust jumping on impact, real physics.
```

**A2. Book slam**
```
Vertical shot: a thick revision guide slams shut on a desk in slow motion, pages compressing, a small shockwave of loose flashcards jumping and fluttering down around it, warm light, crisp detail, decisive energy.
```

**A3. Light switch**
```
A dark teenage bedroom at night; a hand hits the desk lamp switch and warm light snaps across an organised desk in one frame, revealing neat purple folders and a ready past paper. Darkness to order in an instant, macro realism.
```

**A4. The rip**
```
Macro slow motion: two hands tear a page of dense messy notes cleanly down the middle, fibres separating in detail, and behind the tear a clean cream flashcard is revealed in sharp focus. Bright, tactile, a tiny bit dramatic.
```

**A5. Whiteboard wipe**
```
Vertical shot: a hand with a cloth wipes a completely full, chaotic whiteboard in one fast arc, leaving a clean white surface with only a small purple tick drawn in the corner. One motion, before and after in a single second, bright room.
```

**A6. Timer slam**
```
Macro: a hand slaps a chunky kitchen timer down onto a desk and twists it in one motion, the dial spinning to position with a loud mechanical click, shallow focus, warm light, instant business energy.
```

**A7. Cold open exam hall**
```
Wide vertical shot from the back of a silent school exam hall: hundreds of single desks in perfect rows under high windows, one desk in the near foreground with a paper squared and a pen laid straight. No people yet, held breath atmosphere, cool morning light warming at the edges.
```

**A8. Confetti of flashcards**
```
Slow motion vertical: dozens of cream and purple flashcards fall through warm light against a plain wall, tumbling and spinning, and one gold-edged card lands flat and face-down on the desk in sharp focus as the rest blur past.
```

**A9. Head on desk to upright**
```
Real and funny: a sixth former sits up from a face-down desk slump in one motion, hair slightly mad, blinks twice, and pulls a past paper toward them with sudden resolve. Handheld, warm evening light, true teenage energy, no glamour.
```

**A10. The unsubscribe**
```
Vertical macro: a thumb dramatically flicks a phone into a desk drawer, the drawer slides shut with a satisfying wooden thunk, and the hand returns to an open notebook. One continuous shot, warm light, comic finality, screen never visible.
```

## B. Study b-roll (cutaways under voiceover)

**B1. Golden hour grind**
```
A sixth former works at a desk by a window as golden hour light moves across the wall behind them in gentle timelapse, pen steady, posture settling deeper into focus, tea going cold beside them. Warm, honest, aspirational, real skin and fabric texture.
```

**B2. Library long lens**
```
Documentary long lens shot down a university-style library reading room: warm lamps on long oak tables, a scatter of students working, one in a deep purple hoodie centred in the shallow focus plane writing steadily. Hushed atmosphere, dust in light shafts.
```

**B3. Flashcards on the bus**
```
Handheld vertical shot: a student flicks through a small stack of flashcards on the top deck of a moving British bus, morning light strobing through trees outside the window, headphones in, completely absorbed. Real bus interior detail, cards blank to camera.
```

**B4. The blurt page fills**
```
Overhead timelapse macro: a blank A4 page fills edge to edge with fast handwritten scribble texture in real time, the hand moving with urgency, pen switching to purple for ticks and circles in a second pass. All writing illegible texture, warm desk light.
```

**B5. Kitchen table night shift**
```
Wide vertical shot: a teenager works alone at a British kitchen table at 10pm under the extractor-hood light, the rest of the house dark, mug beside them, dog asleep on the floor nearby. Cinematic domestic stillness, warm pool of light in darkness.
```

**B6. Rain on the window**
```
Macro: heavy rain runs down a bedroom window in warm lamp light, and beyond the glass, out of focus, a desk lamp and a working silhouette. Cosy, moody, the outside world switched off. Slow, atmospheric, loopable.
```

**B7. Past paper pile shrinks**
```
Stop-motion feel: a tall stack of past papers on a desk shrinks paper by paper while the completed pile beside it grows, the light moving from morning to lamp-lit evening across the sequence, mug refilling twice. Satisfying progress compression, papers unreadable.
```

**B8. The walk to the exam**
```
Slow motion vertical tracking shot: a student in summer school uniform walks a British school corridor toward double doors with light blazing through the glass, bag gone, just a clear pencil case in hand, face set and calm. Long shadows, dust in light, quiet courage.
```

**B9. Post-exam release**
```
Vertical shot: double doors burst open and a wave of sixth formers pours out into summer air, ties loosened, one throwing an arm around a mate, another just closing their eyes into the sun. Handheld in the crowd, warm flare, pure release, honest faces.
```

**B10. 5:45am alarm choice**
```
Macro in blue pre-dawn light: a phone alarm glows face-up on a bedside table, screen soft and unreadable, a hand lands on it, pauses, then swipes and the bedside lamp clicks on. Cut to feet finding slippers. Understated discipline, no hustle-culture aggression, warm lamp against blue.
```

## C. POV and story scenes

**C1. POV: predicted vs wanted**
```
POV vertical shot: hands pin two small cards to a corkboard, one slightly crumpled, one crisp with a gold edge, then pin a strip of paper bridging them. The camera holds on the bridge. Warm lamp light, tactile corkboard detail, all writing soft.
```
Overlay in post: "The bridge is a system. Not more hours."

**C2. POV: opening the mocks email**
```
POV vertical: hands hold a phone face down on a duvet, thumb tapping the case in thought, breath visible in the pause, then the phone turns over revealing only a warm blurred glow, and the grip tightens slightly. Screen never readable, bedroom lamp light, honest nerves.
```
Overlay in post: "Mocks are data, never destiny."

**C3. The younger sibling**
```
Documentary vertical shot: a Year 9 kid leans in a doorway watching their older sibling revise with flashcards at a tidy desk, then wanders off and returns with paper, quietly copying the setup at the other end of the desk. The older one slides them a spare purple pen without looking up. Warm, funny, tender.
```

**C4. Teaching the wall**
```
Vertical shot: a sixth former stands in their bedroom explaining something to a wall covered in blank sticky notes, gesturing like a teacher with a pen as chalk, catching themselves being dramatic, grinning, carrying on anyway. Lamp light, honest and slightly ridiculous, real teenage bedroom.
```
Overlay in post: "If you can teach it, you know it. Feynman was right."

**C5. The doctor's bookshelf**
```
Slow vertical pan up a bookshelf: A-level revision guides at the bottom shelf, undergraduate medicine textbooks in the middle, and a stethoscope coiled on the top shelf beside a small framed photo turned away. Warm library light, dust motes, the whole route in one shelf. No readable spines.
```
Overlay in post: "Same person. Same method. Different shelf."

**C6. Results day flash-forward**
```
Vertical shot: a Year 12 student at their desk on an ordinary evening looks up at a wall calendar, and the light in the room shifts warm and bright for a moment like an August morning before settling back to lamp-lit normal, expression steadying into intent. Subtle magic realism, no text on the calendar.
```
Overlay in post: "364 ordinary days build one August morning."

**C7. The tutor moment**
```
Warm vertical shot over a shared table: a young tutor slides a page back to a student with one thing circled in purple and the student's face travels from confusion to the exact moment it lands, eyebrows up, small laugh, pen grabbed. Genuine connection, natural light, real skin texture, page unreadable.
```

**C8. Closing the loop**
```
Vertical sequence: a student drops the last completed flashcard onto a finished pile, squares the deck, wraps it with an elastic band, and places it in a shoebox archive beside four other banded decks, closing the lid with a pat. Sunday-evening satisfaction, warm light, tactile.
```
Overlay in post: "Done properly, once a week. That's the whole system."

## D. Myth vs method split-energy shots

**D1. Highlighter vs blurt**
```
One vertical shot in two halves of motion: first, a hand glides a highlighter over a textbook in passive smooth strokes, hypnotic and easy; hard cut mid-shot to the same desk where the book is closed and the hand is scrawling everything it remembers on blank paper, urgent and alive. Same warm light, two completely different energies.
```
Overlay in post: "One of these feels like work. Only one is."

**D2. Ten open tabs vs one page**
```
Vertical macro: a desk drowning in open books, printouts, two devices and sticky notes everywhere, camera tightening claustrophobically; snap cut to the same desk holding one past paper, one pen, one timer, camera relaxing wider. Warm light both sides, chaos to clarity in one cut.
```
Overlay in post: "Revision isn't a collection. It's a rep."

**D3. Midnight hero vs 9am pro**
```
Split-energy vertical: a student slumped over books at 1am in cold blue phone light, eyes glazed; cut to the same student at 9am at a clear desk in morning sun, fresh, working a timed paper with a stopwatch running. Same room, opposite physics. Real skin, honest fatigue vs honest focus.
```
Overlay in post: "The all-nighter is a costume. The morning rep is the job."

**D4. Rereading vs retrieval**
```
Vertical macro two-beat: eyes moving smoothly across a textbook page reflected in a student's glasses, comfortable and passive; cut to the same glasses now reflecting a blank page as the student stares upward mid-struggle, lips moving, dragging a fact up from memory, then writing fast. The struggle is the point. Warm lamp light.
```
Overlay in post: "If it feels easy, it isn't going in."

**D5. Copying notes vs closing gaps**
```
Vertical overhead: a hand copies a textbook paragraph in beautiful mirrored handwriting, serene and pointless; hard cut to the same page where the hand now marks three angry purple circles on a self-test and flips straight to those three topics only. Precision beats beauty. Paper texture rich, writing illegible.
```
Overlay in post: "Pretty notes are a souvenir. Fix the circles."

**D6. Busy vs finished**
```
Vertical shot: a student power-walks around their room tidying, sharpening pencils, refilling water, arranging pens by colour, industrious and avoidant; cut to them sitting down, starting the timer, and the room going still around the single moving pen. Comic then calm, warm light, deeply relatable.
```
Overlay in post: "Motion isn't progress. The timer knows."

## E. Satisfying loops and stationery ASMR

**E1. Flashcard shuffle**
```
Macro loop: hands shuffle a deck of cream flashcards with purple backs like a card sharp, square the deck with two taps on the desk, and fan them in one smooth arc. Crisp paper sounds implied, warm light, tactile perfection, clean loop.
```

**E2. Pen line symphony**
```
Extreme macro loop: a fountain pen draws one slow perfect line of wet purple ink across cream paper, the ink levelling and drying with a soft sheen as the nib exits frame, then a fresh page slides in. Hypnotic, glossy, clean loop.
```

**E3. Sticky note grid**
```
Overhead stop-motion loop: cream and purple sticky notes place themselves one by one into a perfect grid on a white wall, corners crisp, the final note gold, then the grid lifts away in a wave and restarts. Playful precision, soft shadows, no writing.
```

**E4. Timer tick to zero**
```
Macro loop: the final ten seconds of a mechanical kitchen timer, the dial creeping to zero, the ring hammer trembling, and the ding landing as a small burst of confetti-light warmth crosses the frame before resetting. Tension and release in eight seconds, brass and cream detail.
```

**E5. Perfect pencil case**
```
Overhead loop: hands place items into a clear exam pencil case one at a time with deliberate care, two black pens, a purple pen, pencil, ruler, rubber, calculator, each landing in its place, zip closing in one clean pull. Pre-exam ritual as ASMR, bright morning light.
```

**E6. Tea and turn**
```
Macro loop: tea pours into a cream mug in slow motion beside an open book whose page turns in the steam breeze, warm morning window light, drops crowning in the cup, cosy and endless. Clean loop.
```

**E7. The gold star**
```
Macro loop: a hand presses a small gold foil star onto a paper chart of hand-drawn boxes, smooths it with a thumb, and the light catches the foil as the camera drifts to the next empty box. Childhood reward system, played straight and warm. No readable text.
```

**E8. Eraser reveal**
```
Extreme macro loop: an eraser sweeps across pencil scribble and the cleared path reveals crisp cream paper, shavings curling and tumbling in warm side light, a hand brushing them away with one soft stroke. Deeply satisfying subtraction, clean loop.
```
