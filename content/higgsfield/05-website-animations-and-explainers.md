# 05. Website animations and explainers

Two kinds of work here. Section A animates REAL assets with image-to-video, which is the only honest way to put screens or the brand mark in motion (rule 3: never generate fake UI). Section B visualises the method itself. Section C is small garnish motion.

For section A: upload the named file, paste the motion prompt. Keep motion subtle; the asset is the star.

---

## B. Method explainer animations (text-to-video, share the site's visual language)

**B1. Blurt and Fix, 90 minutes**
```
Overhead macro sequence on a cream desk: a hand writes fast and messily filling a blank page from memory, then a second colour arrives, deep purple pen ticking what stands and circling gaps, then the gaps get filled from an open book. Three beats, one shot, warm light, real paper texture, energetic then precise. All writing stays illegible scribble texture.
```
Format: 16:9 and 9:16, 8 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.
Overlay in post: "Blurt and Fix. 90 minutes. Empty your head, find the gaps, close them."

**B2. Active Recall, 45 minutes**
```
Macro loop: a hand flips a cream flashcard held above a small stack, pauses on the blank side as if answering from memory, then turns it to check, small satisfied beat, next card. Rhythmic, tactile, warm lamp light, deep purple card backs with gold edges, faces blank. Steady confident cadence.
```
Format: 16:9 and 9:16, 8 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.
Overlay in post: "Active Recall. 45 minutes. Answer first, check second."

**B3. Spaced Review, 30 minutes**
```
Minimal animation feel: a single cream card sits on deep purple, and soft concentric gold ripples pulse outward from it at lengthening intervals, each ripple wider and calmer than the last, like memory settling deeper. Hypnotic, elegant, precise. No text.
```
Format: 16:9 and 9:16, 8 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.
Overlay in post: "Spaced Review. 30 minutes. Little and often beats late and desperate."

**B4. Timed Paper, 60 minutes**
```
Cinematic macro: an analogue stopwatch on cream paper ticks beside a hand working steadily through an exam paper, shallow focus shifting between the sweeping second hand and the moving pen, warm concentrated light, quiet exam-hall energy at a home desk. Paper content stays soft and unreadable.
```
Format: 16:9 and 9:16, 8 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.
Overlay in post: "One timed paper per subject, every week. Pressure practised is pressure tamed."

**B5. The Open Hour**
```
Warm shot: a teenager closes a folder at a tidy desk, stretches, and the shot follows them to a window seat where they pick up a novel and settle in golden light, completely at ease. The door of the hour left open: rest that is chosen, never guilt. Cosy realism, purple cushion, honest calm. No text.
```
Format: 16:9 and 9:16, 8 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.
Overlay in post: "One Open Hour a day. Light work or a proper break. Your call."

**B6. The capacity cap**
```
Macro still-life animation: water pours steadily into an elegant glass carafe on cream wood and stops exactly at a gold line etched near the top, the pour cutting off cleanly, surface settling to glass-calm. Nothing overflows, ever. Warm light, premium restraint, satisfying precision. No text.
```
Format: 16:9, 6 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.
Overlay in post: "Hard weekly cap. A plan you can actually do beats a fantasy timetable."

**B7. Weakest topic first**
```
Overhead animation feel: five cream cards lie in a row on deep purple felt, one visibly creased and dog-eared; a hand slides the creased card to the front of the row and places two gold coins on it while the pristine cards wait. Resource goes where the weakness is. Tactile, deliberate, warm studio light. No writing.
```
Format: 16:9 and 9:16, 6 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.
Overlay in post: "Your weakest topics get the most sessions. That's the whole trick."

**B8. Parked for next week**
```
Macro sequence: a hand lifts one flashcard from an overfull working stack and places it with care into a small wooden tray labelled by position rather than words, squaring it neatly, tray sliding to the desk's edge, main stack now workable. Respectful, organised, zero guilt energy, warm light, purple and cream cards.
```
Format: 16:9, 6 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.
Overlay in post: "Too much for one week? Topics get parked, never crammed."

**B9. The five scores**
```
Elegant motion design feel on cream: five thin deep purple rings draw themselves clockwise to different degrees of completion, arranged in a gentle arc, the fullest ring flushing gold as it closes. Abstract scoreboard of a revision system, minimal, precise, no labels, no numbers.
```
Format: 16:9 and 1:1, 8 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.
Overlay in post: "Five system scores. The diagnostic measures all of them in 20 questions."

## C. Micro-garnish (short stingers between sections and clips)

**C1. Gold tick stamp**
```
On cream, a gold tick stamps into frame with a soft paper-press wobble and one tiny dust puff, then holds. Half a second of motion, then stillness. Tactile print-shop feel, macro grain. No text.
```
Format: 1:1 and 9:16, 3 seconds. Tool: text-to-video. Punctuation between points in edits.

**C2. Purple underline flick**
```
A hand-drawn deep purple underline draws itself briskly beneath empty space on warm cream paper, with a confident flick at the end, ink drying visibly. Fast, characterful, one second of motion. No words above it, that space is for overlay text.
```
Format: 16:9 and 9:16, 3 seconds. Tool: text-to-video.

**C3. Page corner turn**
```
Extreme macro of a cream page corner lifting and turning toward camera in warm light, paper fibre detail, soft shadow crossing, cut point clean on the turn. A transition wipe made of paper.
```
Format: 16:9 and 9:16, 3 seconds. Tool: text-to-video. Use as a scene transition in every edit.

**C4. Three dots arrive**
```
Minimal animation: three small circles, purple, gold, purple, pop into a neat row on cream one after another with soft spring physics and settle. Clean, friendly, quick. No text.
```
Format: 1:1, 3 seconds. Tool: text-to-video. List-point punctuation for social edits.
