# Facebook / Instagram ad creative: Revision Diagnostic

Written 15 July 2026. Paid cold-traffic campaign to get eyeballs on the free Revision Diagnostic, which captures the email and routes each person to the right paid programme. Student-facing (Waleed's call: not parent-facing, not targeted at under-18s). Voice and proof follow the content-studio skill; approved proof only, no grade-outcome claims.

## How this runs (the setup, so the creative has context)

- **Destination for every ad:** https://alevelaccelerators.com/revision-diagnostic
- **Placements:** run it across Facebook feed, Instagram feed and Reels. Students live on Instagram and Reels, so expect those to carry the campaign; the same copy works on all three.
- **Optimise for the completion, not the click.** The diagnostic already fires a Meta "Lead" event when someone finishes it, so once the pixel is on, set the campaign objective to that event and Meta learns to find people who actually complete it.
- **The video is the strongest asset.** A talking-head clip of Waleed will almost always beat a static image in this niche, and the same clip doubles as an organic Reel.
- **Waleed sets the budget and the audience** (age, location, interests) in Ads Manager. No numbers are guessed here; start with a small daily test you would be comfortable losing, read the cost per completed diagnostic from your own Ads Manager, then scale only what your numbers justify.
- **CTA button:** "Learn more" on all variants (the copy does the real work; a free-tool ad does not need a hard button).

## Three feed ad variants

Each is one A/B angle. Run all three to one small audience, keep the one your cost-per-diagnostic likes, kill the other two.

### Variant A: the retrieval gap (the felt exam moment)

**Primary text:**
You revise for hours. You knew it last night. Then you sit the exam and it's gone.

That isn't you being lazy, and it isn't a knowledge problem. It's a retrieval problem, and almost nobody gets taught the difference.

I'm an NHS doctor and a former A-level student who got into medicine. I built a free diagnostic that finds exactly where your marks are leaking, and what to fix first.

20 honest questions. About 4 minutes. Then a proper report on your revision, not generic advice.

**Headline:** Find where your marks are leaking
**Description:** Free revision diagnostic from Dr Waleed Ahmad, MBBS

### Variant B: the predicted-grades gap (matches the summer page)

**Primary text:**
Your predicted grades decide your offers. And they get set on how you perform right now, not on how hard you're trying.

If you're working hard and still stuck a grade or two below where you need to be, something in your method is quietly costing you marks. Most students never find out what.

I'm a doctor. I can't treat a patient until I know what's wrong, so I built the same thing for revision: a free 4-minute diagnostic that pinpoints your weakest link and what to do about it.

Worth 4 minutes before you lose another month to revision that isn't working.

**Headline:** Predicted grades not where they need to be?
**Description:** A free diagnostic from a doctor who has taught 1,000+ students

### Variant C: the confession (four tiers)

**Primary text:**
Confession: I got into medicine with a revision method that was genuinely bad. I just did more hours than everyone else until it worked. That's not a plan, and it nearly cost me.

Here's what school never taught me. Grades come in tiers: knowing it, recalling it, applying it, then performing under exam pressure. Most students are stuck long before the top and have no idea which tier is failing them.

I'm an NHS doctor now, and I built a free diagnostic that tells you exactly which one is holding your grade down.

4 minutes. Honest questions. A real answer.

**Headline:** Which tier is holding your grade down?
**Description:** Free revision diagnostic. Dr Waleed Ahmad, MBBS.

## Doctor-fronted video script (30 to 45 seconds)

Film once, use as the paid ad and as an organic Reel/Short. Talking head, captions burned on (most people watch on mute), Waleed to camera. Keep it unpolished and honest; a phone camera in good light beats a studio here.

**Hook (0 to 2s)**
- Spoken: "You're not revising wrong because you're lazy. You're revising wrong because nobody taught you how."
- On-screen text: YOU'RE NOT LAZY. YOU WERE NEVER TAUGHT HOW.

**Beat 1, the credibility (2 to 12s)**
- Spoken: "I'm an NHS doctor. Six years ago I sat exactly where you're sitting, working harder than everyone and watching people who did less get better grades."
- On-screen text: NHS doctor. Former A-level student.

**Beat 2, the insight (12 to 26s)**
- Spoken: "Here's what I worked out too late. Reading your notes again feels productive, but the exam doesn't test whether you recognise the answer. It tests whether you can pull it out of an empty page under pressure. Those are two completely different skills."
- On-screen text: RECOGNISING it is not the same as RECALLING it.

**Beat 3, the offer (26 to 38s)**
- Spoken: "Most students never find out which one is failing them. So I built a free diagnostic that does. 20 questions, about 4 minutes, and it shows you exactly where your marks are leaking and what to fix first."
- On-screen text: Free. 4 minutes. Find your leak.

**Close / CTA (38 to 43s)**
- Spoken: "It's free. Tap the button and find your leak before your next exam does."
- On-screen text: Tap "Learn more" to start.

**Shot list:** one continuous talking-head take is fine. If cutting, break on each beat change and drop in a 1-second B-roll of the diagnostic on a phone screen over Beat 3. Subtitles on throughout. Vertical 9:16.

**Caption (for the organic Reel version):**
You knew it yesterday. In the exam it's gone. Here's why, and a free 4-minute tool to find your leak.
