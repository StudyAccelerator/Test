## 2. LinkedIn algorithm, mid-2026 (background agent, sources labelled)

- Reach environment: median post reach 8 to 12 percent of followers (down from 15 to 20 a year before); 2025 saw views fall ~50 percent and engagement ~25 percent (REPORTED, van der Blom Algorithm Insights 2026, 1.3M posts).
- Format engagement benchmarks (REPORTED, same research, treat as directional): document/PDF carousels ~6.6 percent (best; under 10 slides, ~9 is the sweet spot), native video 30 to 90s ~5.6, image plus text ~3.2, text-only ~2 (recovering; avoid back-to-back text-only days).
- Comments weigh roughly 2x reactions; saves are the heavyweight signal (Hootsuite guide updated 14 July 2026, VERIFIED fetch: "a save can drive around five times the reach of a like"); dwell time is a first-class signal; first ~60 minutes is the testing window.
- LinkedIn now runs an interest graph with semantic matching ("360Brew"): it classifies what an account is about and shows posts to interested NON-followers. Small accounts win by staying tight on 2 to 3 topics. Hashtags no longer matter (every top performer in this niche uses zero).
- One external link in the body cut median reach ~18.8 percent (REPORTED, van der Blom 2026). Links in first comment.
- Engagement-bait crackdown 2026: "Comment YES if you agree" style is suppressed; comment-gated delivery of a REAL asset still demonstrably out-distributed everything in this niche as of April/May 2026 (Rumi's 184-comment post). Phrase gates as delivery, never agreement; expect the lever to weaken over time.
- Timing: Buffer (4.8M posts, 2026) found weekday 3 to 8pm now beats mornings; Sprout still shows Tue to Thu late morning. For UK sixth formers the late-afternoon window fits both. Test 4:30pm vs 7 to 8am.
- Personal profiles get on the order of 70 percent more reach than company pages.

## 3. Facebook for UK parents, mid-2026 (background agent, sources labelled)

- Format engagement (VERIFIED fetch, Socialinsider study of 25M posts): text posts 0.20 percent, albums 0.18, Reels 0.18, single images 0.15, **link posts 0.05**. Meta's own data: 97.8 percent of viewed feed content has no external link. Meta is testing a 2-links-per-month cap for unverified Pages (Social Media Examiner, Feb 2026, VERIFIED fetch). Links live in first comments.
- Engagement bait is demoted but genuine advice/recommendation questions are explicitly exempt (Meta policy). Ask real questions; never "comment YES" or "share this".
- Reels are the only surface that reaches non-followers at scale, which matters enormously for a page starting at zero. Native upload only, no TikTok watermarks.
- Boost only a post that has already won organically (reach top-up); use Ads Manager for real conversion campaigns. UK 35 to 54s average 50 minutes a day on Facebook/Messenger (REPORTED, Ofcom/DataReportal).
- Timing consensus zone: Tue to Thu 8 to 11am UK; secondary evening peak worth testing.
- What parents of teens share, ranked by evidence: (1) scripts and checklists ("25 ways to ask your kids how school was" is the category's most famous viral); (2) worry-relief at milestone moments (results day); (3) "am I the only one" normalisation (a Mumsnet thread titled "am I the only parent with a child that refuses to revise for A'Levels?" drew 36 replies of "same here", VERIFIED fetch); (4) system explainers (how predicted grades work is a named gap almost nobody fills); (5) pride and humour (reach but no buyer intent).
- The deep parent motivator in this market, tested by the biggest player (Elevate Education's webinar funnel): "the control and visibility you need to assist your child". Frame the diagnostic exactly there: visibility without nagging.
- UK A-level parent attention is NOT in big Facebook pages: it's Mumsnet (fiercely anti-promotional; be recommendable, never promotional), local recommendation groups, and school-distributed PDFs. Lucy Parsons (closest UK business analogue) sustains a real coaching business from a ~5.5k-like page: precision of audience beats scale in this niche.
- Results day (Thu 13 Aug): schools circulate parent results-day guides in EARLY AUGUST; the share window rewards whoever publishes the calm, useful guide first. The w/c 27 July and w/c 3 Aug packs should build this arc.
