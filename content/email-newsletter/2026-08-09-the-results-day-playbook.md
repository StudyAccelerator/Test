{$name},

This Thursday, 8am, results. If that's you: this Session is yours start to finish, and there's nothing for sale at the end of it. If it's not you this year, read it anyway. It's a preview of a morning you'll meet eventually, and you'll want to have seen the map.

One sentence to hold onto first: Thursday is a sorting morning, not a judgement morning. Whatever the slip says, it sorts you onto a route, and every route has a next move. Calm beats clever on Thursday, and calm comes from knowing the moves in advance. So here they are.

**Wednesday night, ten minutes**

Find the logistics: what time school opens, and whether you're going in. Go in if you possibly can. If it looks rough, teachers fight hardest for the student standing in front of them.

Write three numbers on actual paper: your firm choice's admissions line, your insurance's, and UCAS (0371 468 0468). Paper, because phones pick mornings like Thursday to die.

Then charge the phone anyway, and sleep. The result was set in June. Nothing you feel tonight changes it, so spend the evening on something that isn't this.

**The morning, in order**

The UCAS Hub updates from around 8am and tells you whether your place is confirmed. It won't show the grades themselves; those are on the slip at school.

**Met your firm offer:** confirmed automatically. Nothing to do except thank whoever kept you fed this year. Go celebrate properly.

**Missed firm, met insurance:** your insurance confirms automatically. It's real, and you chose it for a reason. If you're gutted, the options below still exist, but sit with it a day before trading a confirmed place for a maybe.

**Just missed, by one grade or so:** phone your firm before you accept anything else. The script: your name, UCAS ID, course, then "I've missed my offer by one grade and I'd like to ask whether you can still take me." Universities take near-misses more often than students believe, but only from the ones who call. If it's a no, ask about the same course with a foundation year, then move to clearing.

**Missed both:** clearing, and clearing is a market, not a punishment. Tens of thousands of students find a place through it every year, and good courses list spaces on Thursday morning. Same script: name, UCAS ID, grades, "do you have places on [course]?" Have your personal statement open, some will ask about it. And you make the calls, not your parents. Universities will only speak to the applicant.

**Beat your offer:** you can look upward through clearing too, if you want to. Only trade up for a course you actually want more, never for a league table position.

**Grades feel wrong:** talk to your teachers on the day about a review of marking. The school submits it, and marks can move down as well as up, so it's a judgement call for genuinely marginal scripts, not a reflex. If a university place depends on it, say so; the priority deadlines are tight.

**The afternoon**

Whatever happened, do something kind and non-academic. It's an emotional day even when it goes well. And if it didn't go well: one morning sorted you onto a longer route. It didn't measure your worth, and it didn't close the door on the life you wanted. Longer routes arrive at the same places. I've watched it happen too many times to count.

**The clinic**

"My grades are going to be bad. Should I even go in?"

Yes. Go in. A bad-news morning feels radioactive from your bedroom and manageable in a corridor full of people having the same morning. Practically, it's the best move you have: the students who show up get their teachers working the phones for them within the hour. And you don't have to perform being fine. Get the slip, get your people moving, leave when you like.

**High-yield / low-yield**

High-yield: the three phone numbers on paper, Wednesday night.

Low-yield: refreshing the Hub at 6am. It updates when it updates, and sleep is better prep for a phone call you might need to make well at 9.
