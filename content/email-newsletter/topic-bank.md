# Sunday Session topic bank

The forward calendar for issues after the drafted launch run, plus an evergreen reserve. Premises only: each becomes a full issue via the production checklist in the README. Dates assume the weekly Sunday 5pm slot. Re-check any named date against the site and the school calendar at writing time.

## Dated: autumn term 2026

- **Sun 30 Aug · The first week back system.** The weekly operating rhythm for Year 13: same-day ten minute rebuild after each lesson, one timed section per subject per week, the Sunday error log hour. Frames the year as a system installed in week one, not a sprint bolted on in spring. Door: Subject Accelerator, two weeks out.
- **Sun 6 Sep · The Sunday hour.** Teach the weekly self-debrief: thirty minutes error log retest, twenty minutes next-week plan, ten minutes honest scoreboard. Door: Subject Accelerator, one week out, final honest call ("if you're in, be in from session one"). The Study System cohort (Wed 16 Sep) is the second door for method-led readers.
- **Sun 13 Sep · Marks per minute.** Cohort starts today. Exam timing as arithmetic: total marks over total minutes, budget per question, the abandon rule (bank the mark you can get, flag the one you can't, return if time). First tests are imminent; usable immediately. Door: cohort starts today, first session risk-free, recordings from day one; written so it still reads well on Sunday evening after the first sessions have run.
- **Sun 20 Sep · The teacher meeting script.** How to ask for a higher prediction without asking for a favour: the marked-work question, the evidence folder, timing the conversation before the meetings happen. Door: Subject Accelerator (join-late honesty: recordings catch up) or Study System depending on list signals.
- **Sun 27 Sep · The 15 October fortnight.** For medicine, dentistry, vet med and Oxbridge applicants: the final UCAS sprint order (form done, referee warned, prediction conversations finished). For everyone else: why the personal statement is not the thing to polish for six weeks, and what to do instead. Door: scheduler for application-nervous students.
- **Sun 4 Oct · Flashcards, done properly.** Most decks test recognition, not recall: question-first cards, no orphan facts, retire mastered cards, diagrams as blank-label rebuilds. Door: Study System (method season starts as mock talk begins).
- **Sun 11 Oct · The mock countdown plan.** Reverse-plan from the mock timetable: past papers by week, error log as the syllabus, the last 48 hours protocol. Door: Study System or Subject Accelerator by list signals; the Mock Result Debrief tool (roadmap item 4) gets seeded here if on track.
- **Sun 18 Oct · The five day repair.** Half term: audit Monday, two topics per day at Tier 2, error log finale, rest built in. Explicitly not a ten hour a day guilt week. Door: soft, tracker.
- **Sun 25 Oct · Group revision: the truth.** When it works (mutual blurting, mark scheme quizzing, teaching each other under a timer) and when it's social loafing in a nice library. Door: soft.
- **November Sundays · Mock season block.** The night before a mock (rest beats cramming), the morning routine, then post-mocks: the mock debrief method (classify K, R, A, craft; feed the fix plan), which pairs with the Mock Result Debrief tool if shipped. Doors: Study System while method pain is loudest; Subject Accelerator spring cohorts as they're announced.
- **December · The honest December.** What to do with a term's evidence: recalibrate targets, the holiday minimum viable plan, why "I'll fix everything over Christmas" fails and the two-topics-per-subject version that doesn't. Door: diagnostic retake invitation (roadmap item 5) once built.

## Evergreen reserve (slot in when a dated premise dies or news takes a week)

- **The mind blank protocol.** What to do in the ten seconds after your mind goes white in an exam: breathe out, move on, seed recall with the question's own words. Best kept for spring exam season.
- **Command words decoded.** Describe, explain, evaluate, compare: what each pays and the sentence shape it wants. Overlap warning: the subject route's sequence taught "the examiner's dictionary"; re-frame rather than repeat, and say so in the copy.
- **Sleep is revision.** Consolidation happens offline; the all-nighter is borrowing marks from tomorrow at a terrible rate. Pairs well with mock or exam season.
- **The 45 minute session.** Why sessions beat days: decision-free starts (topic chosen the night before), phone in another room, one session one output.
- **Why past papers stop working.** The ceiling effect: papers test, they don't teach, unless every lost mark becomes a classified fix. Bridges naturally to any programme door.
- **The catch-up lie.** "I'll catch up at half term / Christmas / Easter" and why the debt compounds; the amnesty method for starting from where you actually are.
- **Asking for help is a system.** The teacher office-hours script, the specific-question rule (never "I don't get it", always "I get to step two and lose it here"), why strong students ask more, not less.
- **Results day (annual).** Every August: the playbook (SS4) and the morning special (SSX) get rewritten with the year's dates and live clearing news. Never reuse verbatim; the facts move.
