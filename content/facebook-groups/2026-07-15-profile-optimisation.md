# Personal profile optimisation: the exact changes

**Date: 15 July 2026.** The step-by-step optimisation of Waleed's personal Facebook profile (facebook.com/profile.php?id=100030411171547), companion to the personal Facebook strategy in this folder. Waleed gave explicit permission for the session to make these changes directly; this file records what was changed, what each field should say, and the assets used.

The profile's job: it is the landing page a parent sees after reading one of Waleed's comments in a group. It is not a distribution channel (the current followers are school friends). Every change below serves the three-second question a parent asks when they click: who is this, can I trust him, what do I do next.

## The checklist

1. **Bio (done before this session):** "NHS doctor. I've taught 1,000+ A-level students the revision system that turns hard work into A*s." Correct: doctor first, proof, promise. Leave as is.
2. **Cover photo.** The grey void is the single worst thing on the profile. Asset created at `content/graphics-src/profile-assets/personal-cover.html`, rendered to `content/graphics/profile-assets/personal-cover.png` (1640 x 624, brand purple #2E2557, gold #C9A96E, cream #F3EBD8, Georgia serif). Copy: eyebrow "DR WALEED AHMAD | NHS DOCTOR | FOUNDER, A-LEVEL ACCELERATORS", headline "Top grades are a system, not a talent.", sub "I teach A-level students the system schools never do. Free tools at alevelaccelerators.com". This is where "Dr" lives, since the name field cannot hold it. Regenerate with the headless Chrome screenshot command if the copy ever changes.
3. **Birthday visibility: Only me.** Profile > About > Contact and basic info > Basic info: set both the day-month and the year audience to Only me. "Foundation Doctor" should lead the profile, not "born 2001". A parent doing mental arithmetic on his age is a distraction the profile does not need.
4. **Friends list visibility: Only me.** Settings and privacy > Settings > Privacy > "Who can see your friends list?" > Only me. Sixty-eight school friends' faces are noise on a shop window.
5. **Work entry:** About > Work and education > Add a workplace: "A-Level Accelerators" (select the Page so it links), position "Founder", set to Public. This is the bridge from profile to Page.
6. **Website link:** About > Contact and basic info > Websites: add alevelaccelerators.com, Public.
7. **Pinned founder-story post** (text below): post publicly, then "..." menu on the post > Pin post.
8. **Featured section (manual, needs his choice of photo):** three items. A photo of him teaching or the graduation photo; a "Start with the free revision diagnostic" card; one proof item. The Featured section is forced public by Facebook, so it is the proof shelf.

## The pinned founder post (final copy)

Six years ago I was sitting where your child is sitting.

I got into medicine with a revision method that was genuinely terrible. I made it on brute force: 12 hour days, notes copied out three times, a timetable that looked beautiful and did nothing. Nobody taught me how to revise. I'm guessing nobody has taught your child either.

I'm now an NHS doctor, and medicine has a rule I wish someone had applied to my revision: diagnose before you treat. You'd be worried if I prescribed something without examining the patient. Yet that's exactly how most teenagers revise. More hours, more highlighting, more past papers, without ever finding what's actually going wrong.

Since qualifying I've worked with over 1,000 A-level students, and the pattern is nearly always the same. It's not effort. Hard working students stall at a B because nobody taught them the system: find the gaps, practise recall instead of recognition, perform under exam conditions.

So alongside the day job, I teach that system. It's called A-Level Accelerators.

If your child is working hard and the marks aren't following, effort isn't the problem. The system is. And that's fixable.

Any questions, just message me. I'm always happy to talk it through.
