# Why the tutor requests never reach him, and where parents actually ask

**Date: 16 July 2026.** Read live from inside Waleed's logged-in Facebook account (personal profile "Waleed Ahmad"). Every number below was read from the named group's own About page or from Facebook post search on that date. Nothing is estimated. No group was joined, no post or comment was made by the session; the only account interaction was reading, and a notification settings dialog that was opened and cancelled without saving.

Companion to `2026-07-15-facebook-groups-and-parent-competitors.md` (the ranked join list) and `2026-07-15-personal-facebook-strategy.md` (Part 6 of which is the standing script for handling a parent request).

## Part 1: Why he gets no notifications, diagnosed

He is already on the personal profile, so the Page-versus-profile problem from the 15 July file is solved. The cause is different and it is a settings default.

Opened the notification settings for "I am looking for a tutor or I want to tutor in the UK" (one of his joined groups). The state:

- **In-app notifications: "Highlights"**, which Facebook's own dialog defines as **"Friends' posts and suggested posts"**.
- **Push notifications: "Highlights"**, defined as **"Suggested posts"**.

That is the whole answer. Lucy Albert is not his friend. No parent posting a tutor request in a public group is his friend. Under Highlights, Facebook was never going to tell him, and it never has. Every request he has ever "stumbled upon" he found by chance, and every one he did not stumble upon he never saw.

Two further findings that shape the fix:

1. **He is in 81 groups** (read from the Groups you've joined page). Switching all 81 to "All posts" would be unusable: three of his groups alone post 227, 307 and 146 times a day. The setting has to go on a shortlist, not the estate.
2. **Push cannot do "All posts".** In that dialog the push options are only "Highlights" or "Off". So even at in-app "All posts", the realistic signal is the red counter on the bell icon, not a phone alert. This matters because the whole value of the play is speed, and a bell counter he checks twice a day is not speed.

## Part 2: The lead radar (better than notifications)

Facebook post search finds live tutor requests **across groups he is not a member of**, in near-real time. Searching `looking for an A level tutor` on 16 July returned Lucy's post at 23 minutes old, alongside requests from five other groups.

Bookmark these as the daily sweep:

- `facebook.com/search/posts?q=looking%20for%20an%20A%20level%20tutor`
- `facebook.com/search/posts?q=looking%20for%20a%20maths%20tutor%20A%20level`
- `facebook.com/search/posts?q=%22A%20level%22%20tutor%20recommendations%20daughter%20son`

Vary the subject and the phrasing (biology, chemistry, physics, "year 12", "year 13", "need a tutor"). Results skew recent but are not strictly sorted by time, and Facebook offers no saved-search alerting, so this is a manual sweep, not an alert. Two or three sweeps a day beats notifications on every count.

**What the sweep immediately proved: parents cross-post the same request to several groups.** Lucy's identical message was live in **"Online Tutoring UK"** (6 comments) and **"GCSE and A-Level UK tutoring"** (3 comments, including his). So "I am the second person to reply" was true of one copy only; across both copies he was one of about nine. Always check whether a request is cross-posted before judging the competition.

## Part 3: Where parents actually ask, measured

Three distinct group types produce tutor requests, and they need three different plays.

### Type A: the tutor-spam firehoses (real demand, brutal competition)

| Group | Members | Posts/month | Posts today | Growth |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Online Tutoring UK | 29.0K | 8,493 | 307 | +964/week |
| GCSE and A-Level UK tutoring | 27.2K | 6,918 | 227 | +835/week |
| I am looking for a tutor or I want to tutor in the UK | 69.9K | 2,769 | 146 | +276/week |

He is already in all three. Genuine parent requests land here constantly, but so does the same copy-paste agency pitch: "Rana Roy" of SureWinPath was observed answering Lucy's post and an unrelated economics request with a byte-identical brochure comment. A request collects competing replies within about 15 minutes and the post itself sinks under hundreds of others within the hour. **Play: speed only.** These are worth sweeping, not living in.

One thing to fix here: a founder-story post of his ("Six years ago, I was sitting exactly where your child is sitting right now...") is **stuck in the pending-approval queue** of GCSE and A-Level UK tutoring. That group has one admin (Harriet) against 227 posts a day, so the queue is effectively a black hole. Do not wait on it.

### Type B: the parent-recommendation groups (the real prize)

**GCSE & A Level Resources UK** (public, 2,102 members, +45/week, created February 2025, group id 1157832682617885). Only 15 posts in the last month, 9 of them today, so it is bursty rather than busy. Rule 3 bans self-promotion outright. He is already a member.

The reason it matters is what the requests look like inside it. An anonymous parent posted "Looking for a patient GCSE Maths tutor. My son understands topics in class but struggles applying them in exams" and drew 19 comments. Another drew 11. The replies are **parents recommending tutors to each other by name** ("Aadam Mulla great maths tutor", "We have an absolutely brilliant tutor for mine who was struggling exactly like that"), not agencies pitching. That is a different economy: you do not win by replying fastest, you win by being the person other parents name. It also runs Facebook's anonymous-posting feature, which is the tell of a group where parents feel safe admitting their child is struggling.

Note the admins are **Bella and Chloe**, the same team that runs **GCSE resources UK** (73.5K members, Tier 1 in the 15 July research). Standing earned in one plausibly carries.

**Play:** live here. This is where the Top Contributor badge and the recommendation habit are worth the months they take.

### Type C: local community groups (the surprise, with a catch)

The `"A level" tutor recommendations` sweep returned real, well-formed, year-group-specific A-level maths requests from **local town groups**, none of which are tutoring groups and none of which he is in:

- "I LIVE IN ASHTEAD": "Looking for an A level Maths tutor for my son presently year 12, preferably face to face." One reply, and it was a link to a tutor directory.
- "Streetlifers of Stourbridge and All Surrounding Areas": "Looking for recommendations for an experienced A level edexcel maths tutor for my daughter currently in year 12."
- "Banstead Today": "Looking for an A Level Maths Tutor for my daughter in year 13."
- Plus GCSE-level equivalents in "Stanway Community Group" and "Colchester Community Group (UK)".

These parents write in full sentences, name the year group and the exam board, and get almost no tutor competition. The catch is real and it is twice over: they are geographically scattered, so there is no single group to join, and several explicitly ask for **face to face**, which he does not do. Treat as a sweep target rather than a join target: the post search reaches them without membership, and an online offer is worth a reply to the ones who did not specify face to face.

## Part 4: The honest constraint under all of this

Most of the volume found in every sweep was **GCSE**, which he does not sell. The A-level maths requests were real but sparse: roughly one an hour across all of UK Facebook on a mid-July Thursday, and mid-July is the post-exam lull. Group request-answering is a genuine channel and it is nearly free, but on this evidence it is a trickle, not a pipeline. It is worth doing because it costs minutes and teaches parent language; it is not worth mistaking for the growth plan. The plays in Part 3 of the 15 July research (the free human conversation, the founder-fronted parent content, the owned A-level parents group) remain the bigger prize.

## Part 5: Standing rules for this channel

Unchanged from the 15 July files, restated because they bind every session:

- Waleed joins groups, posts and comments himself. Sessions read only, never join, post, comment, react or DM as him.
- Sessions never change his account or notification settings; they report what the settings are and he changes them.
- Group rules are read before anything is drafted for that group. Several of these groups ban links, self-promotion, or both.
