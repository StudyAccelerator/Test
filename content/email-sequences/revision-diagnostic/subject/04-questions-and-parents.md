**"Isn't my predicted grade basically decided already?"**

Mostly not, and the timing is the point. Predicted grades get set and reviewed in the autumn term, off the evidence teachers have in front of them: early Year 13 tests, mocks, classwork. A student who spends September to November producing exam-quality answers gives their teacher new evidence exactly when it counts. I can't promise what any teacher decides, and I won't. What I can say is that predictions follow evidence, and autumn is when the evidence gets written. Waiting until after mocks to fix exam craft is answering the question after it's been asked.

**"Will it clash with school, sport, life?"**

It's built not to. Maths is Saturday 1pm to 3pm, Biology Sunday 10am to 12pm, Chemistry Sunday 1pm to 3pm. Nothing touches the school week, and the subjects never overlap each other. If a weekend goes wrong, the session's recorded. Between sessions, budget two to three hours of independent work: mostly the drill you already run, now with your coached answers feeding it.

**"I'm one person in a group. How does that fix MY wording?"**

Fair challenge, and it's about the design. Groups are kept small on purpose, and the sessions are built around answers, yours included, being worked on against the scheme in real time. Here's the thing students don't expect: watching someone else's answer get re-worded teaches you almost as much as your own, because everyone's W gaps rhyme. The same missing "because", the same vague verb. You'll hear your own mistakes coming out of other people's answers within two sessions, and that's when the wording starts fixing itself mid-exam.

**"£339 feels like a lot."**

It's real money, so here's the honest scale. The UK average for 1:1 A-level tutoring is about £50 an hour, so £339 buys under seven hours of it. The Accelerator is twelve weeks of weekly live specialist sessions for the same money. If it's still out of reach, everything in my last three emails, the drill, the letters, the dictionary, costs nothing and works. The programme buys you the examiner's eyes and the weekly structure. It doesn't buy secrets.
