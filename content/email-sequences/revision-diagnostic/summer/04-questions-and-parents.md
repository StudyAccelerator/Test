**"What if I miss sessions? We're away in August."**

Every session is recorded. If you're in Portugal for a week, you watch the two recordings by the pool and you've lost nothing except the chance to ask live questions that week. Students do this every summer. What I'd say is: don't join planning to watch everything as recordings. The live room is where the coaching happens, where your answer gets corrected rather than someone else's. Recordings are the safety net, not the programme.

**"I did fine in Year 12. Is this for people who are behind?"**

No, and honestly this is the myth I most want to kill. Plenty of students join already doing well because they want September to start with them in front. Fine in Year 12 and ready for Year 13 are different claims: your own diagnostic put {$diag_bottleneck|default('one of your five systems')} in the leak zone, and you're exactly the kind of student I mean. The gap between where you are and your target of {$diag_target_grade|default('your target grade')} doesn't close by feeling fine in July. It closes with evidence, and the autumn tests that set your predicted grades arrive faster than anyone expects.

**"How much of my summer does it actually take?"**

Per subject: two live sessions a week, two hours each. Plus, if you're doing it properly, your 90 minute days on your own weak-topic list in between. Call it eight to ten focused hours a week for one subject, with whole afternoons and most days left completely alone. The Accelerator is designed around the same principle I gave you last week: a summer needs rest in it or it wasn't a summer. Anyone who tells you to revise seven hours a day in July is selling burnout.

**"I hate speaking in groups."**

You never have to touch a microphone. Chat questions get answered the same as spoken ones. Most students lurk for the first session and forget to be shy by the third, but nobody is forced anywhere. The groups are small on purpose, so the tutor sees everyone's work either way.

**"Can I join for just one subject?"**

Yes. £289 for one. Most students start with the subject their diagnostic flagged and add a second later if the first one earns it. Sessions for different subjects never clash, so bundles work timetable-wise if you want them.
