The diagnostic put {$diag_child_name|default('your child')}'s biggest leak in **{$diag_bottleneck|default('the method itself')}**, which means something important for you: this is not a subject problem, so a subject tutor is probably not the first fix.

Here is the pattern from the outside. They work, sometimes a lot. The grades do not follow. So the household concludes more work is needed, the pressure goes up, they work more, and the grades still do not follow. Everyone gets more tired and nothing moves. Sound familiar?

The reason is that revision has a machine under it: how you learn something, when you return to it, how you check it stuck. If the machine is wrong, every hour fed into it leaks. Their diagnostic estimated {$diag_low_yield_hours|default('a real share')} of {$diag_hours_per_week|default('their')} weekly hours are currently going into work that feels productive, reading, highlighting, making notes look right, but scores almost nothing. More hours into that machine just leaks more.

**Tonight's question**, asked with genuine curiosity, not as a trap: "when you revise something, how do you check it actually stuck?" If the answer is any version of "I go over it again", you have heard the leak out loud. The fix is testing themselves with the book closed, and the report's 7 day plan starts exactly there.
