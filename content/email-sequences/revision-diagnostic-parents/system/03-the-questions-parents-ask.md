Four questions I hear about the Study System, answered straight.

**"Shouldn't they just discipline themselves?"** The research and my thousand-plus students say the same thing: willpower is not a plan. Students who revise consistently do not have more discipline, they have fewer decisions. The session is already decided, the topic is already decided, the method is already decided. That is what a system means. Expecting a seventeen year old to invent that alone, mid Year 13, under pressure, is how most households end up in the nagging loop.

**"What evidence is there?"** No grade claims from me until our first results land in August, and I would side-eye anyone in this industry who claims otherwise. What I can show: measured topic confidence across our recent subject cohorts moved from 6.2 to 8.3 out of 10 across 74 responses. And the method itself, retrieval practice and spaced repetition, is the most replicated finding in the learning science literature. I did not invent it. I teach students to actually run it.

**"Why not just get a tutor?"** A tutor improves one subject while the underlying method keeps leaking in all of them. Fix the method and every subject moves, including the ones you were never going to pay for tutoring in. And if there already is a tutor, the system makes those expensive hours finally stick.

**"What if they resist the whole idea?"** Then start free and small: the one question from my first email, plus the tracker. Buying anything for a student who has not agreed to it is burning money. If they run the free week and feel the difference, the conversation changes on its own.
