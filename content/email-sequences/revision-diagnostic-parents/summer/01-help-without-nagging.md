{$name},

Quick question. What happens when you ask {$diag_child_name|default('your teenager')} "have you revised today?"

If the answer is a grunt, a door, or "yes" with no evidence, you are in the most common trap in this house and every other one. The question feels like support from your side and surveillance from theirs. And it measures the wrong thing anyway: time in the bedroom is not marks in the exam. The diagnostic already showed you that.

Three swaps that actually work:

**Swap "have you revised?" for "what did you get wrong today?"** Getting things wrong is the whole engine of good revision. If they got nothing wrong, they revised nothing hard. Asking about mistakes makes mistakes normal, and it tells you in ten seconds whether the work was real.

**Swap checking up for a fixed slot.** Agree one weekly ten minute check-in, Sunday works, where they show you their plan for the week. Not the work itself, the plan. You are auditing the system, not the child. The free tracker builds the plan for them: https://alevelaccelerators.com/revision-tracker

**Swap praise for effort with praise for method.** "You did four hours" rewards sitting there. "You tested yourself with the book closed" rewards the thing that scores.

Why this matters now: {$diag_child_name|default('your child')} is going into Year 13, and the summer is the one stretch where the method can be rebuilt without term-time pressure. More on that in a few days. Tonight, just try the first swap.
