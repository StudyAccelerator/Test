The same four questions come up on nearly every parent call about the Summer Accelerator. Here they are with the answers I give on the phone.

**"Will it move their grade?"** Honest answer: I do not publish grade claims, because our first cohort's results arrive this August and I will not invent numbers before then. Anyone in this industry promising you a grade is selling, not teaching. What I can show you: across our recent twelve week Biology and Chemistry programmes, students rated their topic confidence before and after every session, and the average went from 6.2 out of 10 to 8.3, across 74 responses. Confidence is not a grade, but it is measured, and it is mine to show you.

**"Is six weeks online really better than a tutor?"** Different job. A tutor patches one subject, one hour at a time, at about £50 an hour. The Accelerator gets ahead of the whole Year 13 syllabus in their key subjects and trains the method while doing it. Some families run both. If money forces a choice, the summer window favours the course, because the timing cannot be bought back later.

**"More screen time, seriously?"** Live and interactive, not passive video. They answer questions, they get asked things by name, and sessions are capped small so hiding is hard. It is closer to a classroom than to YouTube.

**"What if they will not engage?"** Then do not enrol them, and reply to this email instead. There is usually a conversation that needs to happen before any course will land, and I would rather help you have that one for free than take your money for the wrong week.
