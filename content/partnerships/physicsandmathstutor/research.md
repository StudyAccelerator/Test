# Partnership dossier: Physics & Maths Tutor (PMT Education)

Researched 13 July 2026 with the partnership-outreach process. Status: awaiting Waleed's review. Nothing has been sent. Every number below either carries its source or is marked as not knowable from outside. Sources with links are listed at the end.

## The short version

PMT is the biggest free A-level and GCSE resource site in the country, run as a genuine non-profit by its founder, Görkem Çavlı, who is the sole company director and the person to reach. Their own advertising page claims 9.6 million average monthly page views and 560,000 average monthly unique users. They are exceptional at materials (notes, past papers, questions by topic) and thin on method: revision technique exists on PMT only as unbylined blog articles. No taught study-skills product, no live free teaching, no interactive tools, no expert face on the method side. Their paid arms (crash courses, one-to-one tuition, site advertising) are all subject-content shaped too.

The recommended play: a free, live, co-promoted revision systems series for PMT students in early September, method only, no selling, PMT keeps the recordings. It fits their stated mission, costs them almost nothing beyond promotion, gives their channels something none of their competitors offer, and gives Waleed reach into the largest A-level audience in the UK plus consented registrant emails.

## How the money works

All of it funds the non-profit; none of it is a study-skills product.

- **Revision courses:** online crash courses via Google Classroom and Google Meet, recorded for later viewing. Most three-day courses cost £245; single-day specialist days (Mechanics, Statistics) £95. They run at Christmas, Easter, May half term, exam season and summer; this year's summer refreshers run 18 to 25 August. GCSE and A-level, with IB mentioned, plus university admissions prep (for example Oxford PAT preparation). Taught by "70+ top-rated tutors", described as qualified teachers and expert tutors.
- **One-to-one tuition:** a "Find a Tutor" platform from £20 per hour, tutors interviewed and hand-picked. (This is the marketplace model Waleed deliberately avoids for his own brand; do not mirror their language for his side.)
- **Advertising:** banner ads and sponsored notes, sold via their contact form. Outside brands already appear on their pages, so a partner's name on PMT is not culturally alien to them.
- **Pearson:** their A-level Maths solution banks are Pearson material, so they already hold working arrangements with big education brands.

## The gap, pressure-tested

Claiming "PMT has nothing on revision technique" would be false and would cost credibility with Görkem. The honest picture:

**What they already do on method.** A blog with revision-technique articles: "How to revise effectively: 10 tips for exam success" (covers active recall and spaced repetition), a mock exams article that teaches blurting, "How to make a revision timetable you'll actually use!", a revision apps roundup, and parent-facing pieces ("Revision techniques that work: a parent's guide"). The blog spans students, parents, teachers and tutors, with 100+ articles, mostly unbylined.

**What they verifiably do not do:**

- No taught study-skills product anywhere on the ladder. The courses are subject content only; there is no method course, and no free live teaching of any kind was found.
- No interactive tools. Their own "complete guide to using the site" lists no planner, no diagnostic, no tracker, no quiz engine.
- No expert face on method. The articles are staff-written; nobody owns "how to revise" at PMT the way Görkem owns the resources story.
- No email moat: one monthly newsletter.

**The honest gap statement:** PMT covers revision technique as content but does not teach it as a product. The difference between an article that mentions active recall and a doctor live-teaching a hall of students how to run their revision week is the whole difference. Waleed's business is built on exactly that difference, and their audience's next problem after downloading the notes is exactly this one.

## What PMT plausibly wants (inference, labelled as such)

- **Mission delivery.** The constitution says level the playing field. Materials are now level; method is not. Students at strong schools get taught how to study, most others do not. A free method series for their audience sits squarely inside their stated purpose.
- **Differentiation in a crowded market** (MyTutor, Save My Exams, Seneca and the rest): "PMT students also get taught how to revise, live, by a doctor, free" is a line no competitor has.
- **Engagement between downloads.** Their model is arrive, download, leave. Live events, recordings and a series give their blog, newsletter and socials something substantial, especially in September when exam traffic is quiet.
- **Social and newsletter growth.** A live series converts page visitors into followers and subscribers; their social presence is small relative to their traffic.

## The recommended trade: the Revision Systems series

**Shape.** Two or three free live sessions for PMT students in early September, the habit-setting fortnight of the school year, taught by Waleed. Session territory: setting up the year's revision system, retrieval and spacing in practice, running a real week (audit, plan, review). Method only, no subject teaching, and a written no-selling rule: Waleed does not pitch paid programmes in-session.

**PMT's side of the trade.** Promote it (newsletter, a slot on their own site, socials, one blog post), approve the content, and keep the recordings for their channels with Waleed's byline and a link.

**Waleed's side.** Build the registration page, the decks and the delivery. Run the sessions. Hand over the recordings.

**What Waleed gets.** Reach into the largest A-level audience in the country at the best method-teaching moment of the year; registrant emails with explicit consent to hear from both PMT and him; PMT's name alongside his (with their approval) as the third big-brand delivery credit after The Medic Life and PasTest; and a first yes that opens later doors (tool listings, course bolt-on below).

**What PMT gets.** A mission-true free product they do not have, delivered by a practising doctor with a proven record in exactly this format, for the cost of some promotion slots and a sign-off call.

**Why now.** Pitch mid-July. Plan in August around their summer courses (18 to 25 August) and results days (A-level Thursday 13 August, GCSE Thursday 20 August). Deliver early September, when every Year 12 and 13 in the country is setting the habits they will carry to next summer.

**Objections, answered before the call:**

He is not asking PMT to promote anything paid.

His record is checkable: over 250 students in 10 weeks for The Medic Life, a PasTest partnership, free workshops for r/A-levels with outstanding reviews, and two live free tools (diagnostic, tracker) that prove the method is productised, not theory.
- *"Brand risk."* They approve everything before it goes out; sessions can run on their infrastructure if they prefer; his public materials and testimonials are checkable in minutes.
- *"Effort."* One sign-off call plus promotion slots. He does the rest.
